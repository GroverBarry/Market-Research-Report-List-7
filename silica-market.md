<p><p>The silica market primarily consists of three types: quartz, tridymite, and cristobalite. Quartz, the most abundant, is widely used in glass, ceramics, and construction materials. Tridymite, less common, is essential in high-temperature applications, especially in the semiconductor industry. Cristobalite, with its high thermal stability, is utilized in advanced ceramics and coatings. These silica varieties cater to diverse industries, influencing their market dynamics based on demand for specific properties such as purity, particle size, and thermal resistance.</p></p>
