<p><p>The stearic acid market can be categorized into three primary types: fractionation, pressing method, and hydrogenation method. Fractionation involves separating stearic acid from triglycerides through controlled heating and cooling, yielding high-purity products. The pressing method extracts stearic acid from plant oils via mechanical pressing, producing crude oils and fats. Hydrogenation transforms unsaturated fatty acids into saturated forms, enhancing stability and shelf life. Each method caters to diverse industrial applications, including cosmetics, food, and pharmaceuticals, emphasizing purity and performance.</p></p>
