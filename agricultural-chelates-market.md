<p><p>The Agricultural Chelates Market includes several types of chelating agents used to enhance nutrient availability in plants. EDTA (ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid) is commonly used for its effectiveness in binding metals like iron and zinc. EDDHA (ethylenediamine-N,N'-bis(2-hydroxyphenylacetic acid)) is preferred for alkaline soils due to its stability. DTPA (diethylenetriaminepentaacetic acid) is effective in neutral to alkaline conditions. IDHA (iminodiacetic acid) aids in metal mobility and plant absorption. Other categories encompass various innovative chelates tailored for specific agricultural needs.</p></p>
