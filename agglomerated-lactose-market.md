<p><p>The agglomerated lactose market includes two primary types: lactose monohydrate and lactose anhydrous. Lactose monohydrate is a crystalline form that contains one molecule of water, enhancing its solubility and making it ideal for pharmaceutical applications and food products. Conversely, lactose anhydrous is a dehydrated form, offering reduced moisture content, increased stability, and improved flow properties, which are advantageous in tablet production and powdered formulations. Both types serve diverse industries, including pharmaceuticals, food, and nutraceuticals.</p></p>
