<p><p>Radial forging machines are categorized into hot forging and cold forging markets based on the temperature at which the metal is processed. Hot forging involves shaping metals at elevated temperatures, which enhances ductility and allows for complex geometries, often used for large components in industries like automotive and aerospace. Conversely, cold forging occurs at room temperature, offering tighter tolerances and improved surface finish, making it ideal for producing smaller, high-strength components in various applications. Both methods cater to specific industry needs.</p></p>
