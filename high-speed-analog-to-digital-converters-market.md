<p><p>The high-speed analog-to-digital converters (ADCs) market is categorized into two main types: high-speed and ultra-high-speed. High-speed ADCs typically offer sampling rates ranging from tens to hundreds of megasamples per second, making them suitable for applications like telecommunications and video processing. In contrast, ultra-high-speed ADCs achieve sampling rates in the gigasamples per second range, catering to advanced applications such as radar, medical imaging, and high-frequency communication systems. Both types play critical roles in digital signal processing and data acquisition.</p></p>
