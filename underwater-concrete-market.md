<p><p>The underwater concrete market is segmented into three main types: cement, aggregates, and admixtures. Cement serves as the binding material, providing structural integrity, while aggregates—such as sand, gravel, or crushed stone—offer bulk and strength. Admixtures are chemical additives that enhance properties like workability, setting time, and durability when submerged. Together, these components create specialized concrete formulations capable of withstanding the unique challenges of underwater construction, including water pressure and potential erosion.</p></p>
