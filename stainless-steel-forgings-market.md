<p><p>The stainless steel forgings market comprises several types: castings, hot/cold forged parts, and sintered parts. Castings involve pouring molten stainless steel into molds to create complex shapes. Hot forged parts are shaped at elevated temperatures, enhancing ductility and reducing brittleness, while cold forged parts are formed at room temperature, resulting in improved strength through strain hardening. Sintered parts are produced by compacting stainless steel powder and heating it below its melting point, allowing for intricate designs and tailored properties.</p></p>
