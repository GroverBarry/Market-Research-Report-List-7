<p><p>The anterior chamber market primarily includes three key material types for intraocular lenses: PMMA (Polymethyl Methacrylate), Silicone, and Hydrophobic Acrylate. PMMA lenses are rigid and durable, often used in more traditional applications. Silicone lenses offer flexibility and better biocompatibility, making them suitable for a wider range of patients. Hydrophobic Acrylate lenses are favored for their excellent optical properties and reduced risk of posterior capsule opacification, providing enhanced performance in cataract surgery. Each material caters to specific clinical needs and patient preferences.</p></p>
