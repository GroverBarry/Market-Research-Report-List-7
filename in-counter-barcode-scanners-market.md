<p><p>The in-counter barcode scanners market is primarily divided into two types: one-dimensional (1D) and two-dimensional (2D) barcode scanners. One-dimensional scanners read linear barcodes, which consist of parallel lines and varying widths, commonly found on retail products. In contrast, two-dimensional scanners can decode complex barcodes, such as QR codes, that store more information in a grid format, allowing for enhanced data capture and faster processing. Both types are vital for efficient checkout processes and inventory management in retail environments.</p></p>
