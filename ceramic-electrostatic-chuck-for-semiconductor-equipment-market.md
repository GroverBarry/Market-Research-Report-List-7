<p><p>Ceramic electrostatic chucks (ESCs) are essential components in semiconductor manufacturing, facilitating precise wafer handling. The Coulomb type utilizes electrostatic forces generated by charged ceramic surfaces to hold the wafer securely, making it suitable for various applications. In contrast, the Johnsen-Rahbek (JR) type employs a dielectric layer that enhances the gripping force through electrostatic principles, allowing for improved stability and alignment. Both types contribute to the efficiency and precision required in advanced semiconductor processes.</p></p>
