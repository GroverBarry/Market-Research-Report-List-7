<p><ul><li>Normal Phase HPLC</li><li>Reverse Phase HPLC</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Analytical HPLC systems are classified into two main types: normal phase HPLC and reverse phase HPLC. Normal phase HPLC utilizes a polar stationary phase and a non-polar mobile phase, making it suitable for separating compounds based on polarity. In contrast, reverse phase HPLC employs a non-polar stationary phase and a polar mobile phase, ideal for separating hydrophobic compounds. Both types cater to diverse applications in pharmaceuticals, environmental analysis, and food safety, driving growth in the analytical HPLC systems market.</p></p>
