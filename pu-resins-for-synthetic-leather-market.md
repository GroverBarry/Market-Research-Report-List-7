<p><p>PU resins for synthetic leather are crucial in the production of both dry-process and wet-process synthetic leather. </p><p>Dry-process synthetic leather involves the application of PU resins onto a fabric substrate, utilizing a solvent-free method that results in a lightweight and durable material. </p><p>In contrast, wet-process synthetic leather employs a coating technique that creates a thicker and more flexible product by using water-based PU resins, leading to enhanced softness and a more leather-like texture. Both types cater to diverse consumer preferences and applications.</p></p>
