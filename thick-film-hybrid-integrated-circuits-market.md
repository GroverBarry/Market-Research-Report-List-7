<p><p>The thick-film hybrid integrated circuits market is categorized by substrate types, which include Al2O3 ceramic substrates, BeO ceramic substrates, AlN substrates, and others. Al2O3 (alumina) is favored for its excellent electrical insulation and thermal stability. BeO (beryllium oxide) substrates offer superior thermal conductivity but are less commonly used due to safety concerns. AlN (aluminum nitride) substrates have high thermal conductivity and good dielectric properties. Other substrates may include specialized materials catering to specific application needs, enhancing performance or reducing costs.</p></p>
